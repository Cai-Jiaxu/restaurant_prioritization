# Problem Statement: Restaurant Marketing Prioritization

> *Design a data-driven system that builds a prioritized list of restaurants to market, using both current performance and emerging trends to optimize marketing efforts.*

## The Core Problem

Hungry Hub currently lacks a systematic, data-driven approach to allocate marketing resources across its partner restaurants. The current strategy is reactive rather than predictive, resulting in suboptimal marketing spend and missed opportunities to capitalize on restaurants with high growth potential or emerging momentum.

## Context & Key Pain Points

### Revenue Concentration Risk
The current portfolio is heavily skewed, with just 20% of partners driving 98% of revenue. Any dip in performance among these top performers poses a major risk to the bottom line. Relying solely on these established heavy hitters ignores the stability that a more diversified portfolio would provide.

### Missed "Hidden Gems"
There is a lack of visibility into "hidden gems"—restaurants with high ratings and quality but low current traffic. Without a system to identify them, Hungry Hub cannot scale these partners into future revenue drivers.

### Inefficient Resource Allocation
Without a clear classification system, marketing teams cannot identify which restaurants deserve investment at a glance. Resources may be wasted on "Declining" restaurants that need operational fixes rather than marketing, or "Stable" restaurants that are already at capacity, while "Rising" stars are starved of attention.

## Project Objective

The goal is to design a **recommendation dashboard** powered by advanced analytics and machine learning to automatically identify restaurant segments (Rising, Stable, Declining, Hidden Gem).

The solution must:

1. **Detect Momentum**: Analyze indicators such as booking volume, review trends, and seasonality to uncover early signs of growth.
2. **Optimize ROI**: Recommend where marketing resources should be focused to maximize bookings and revenue.
3. **Provide Actionable Intelligence**: Shift the marketing strategy from reacting to past booking data to predicting future high-value partners.
